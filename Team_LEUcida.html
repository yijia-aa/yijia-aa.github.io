April ~ October 2020

Project LEUcida is a student-initiated Value-In-Action project dedicated to supporting leukemia and lymphoma patients in cooperation with the Leukemia and Lymphoma Foundation in Singapore.

Through various online activities such as contactless handicraft, online concert and merchandise sales, this project aims to raise funds for leukemia and lymphoma patients as well as create public awareness about the diseases, in order to improve patientsâ€™ welfare.

We recruited student volunteers who actively signed up for different roles in the project. Thanks to their unwavering support, we successfully carried out the activities. In the end, we raised $1985 and reached up to around 1,000 audiences.

My major learning point through this one-year project is that there can numerous ways for students like me to do charity work, as far as I want to do it. Previously, my stereotypical view on charity work done by students in Singapore is to stand beside the street, hold aluminium cans and ask for donations from passersby.

However, aside from that, we are able to participate in plenty of activities held by charity websites and local charities. We can look for and sign up for all these activities on giving.sg or volunteer.sg.
